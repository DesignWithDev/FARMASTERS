Description:

The primary centre of origin of black gram is India and its secondary origin is central Asia. It is an important pulse crop in India, Pakistan, Myanmar and parts of southern Asia, Africa and America

Distribution

In India, it is primarily grown in Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Orissa, Andhra Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh, and Tamil Nadu

Climatic Conditions Required for Gram Production:

gram is basically a warm weather crop. It comes up in areas receiving annual rainfall of 600 to 1000 mm. It is grown from sea level up to an altitude of 1800 m.

It is grown on a variety of soil ranging from sandy to heavy black cotton soils. It prefers water retentive heavy soils. Well drains heavy soils with a pH ranging from 5.0 to 7.5 appear to be ideal. It cannot stand saline or alkaline conditions. Land preparation is similar to that for green gram.

Growth and Developmental Processes of Barley:

To a very limited extent, black gram is cultivated as irrigated crop during summer, especially after the harvest of rabi crop. In Peninsular a.id south India, black gram usually is grown after rabi rice. April second fortnight is optimum time of seeding.
